1. General Information

Welcome to the Modular Tools system of TheDifferential. Create custom tools by combining three parts—each made of different materials for unique effects.


How It Works:

  • Head - Defines tool type, mining speed, attack power, and base durability
  • Binding - Connects parts together and adds durability plus modifiers
  • Handle - Affects overall durability multiplier

Material Traits:

Every material brings a passive effect. Stack materials strategically to create the perfect tool for your needs.

2. Tool Crafting & Smelting

Step 1: The Forge Furnace (Multiblock Smeltery)

Forge Setup:

  1. Blaze Burner Layer (Y0): A 3x3 layout of 9 Blaze Burners (Heated +1 lvl , Seething +2 lvl).
  2. Controller Layer (Y1): 1 Forge Controller block placed on the front face, surrounded by 8 Forge Bricks (forming a 3x3 layer).
  3. Glass Layers (Y2-17): 3x3 layers of Forge Glass blocks. You can stack up to 16 forge glass layers.

Scaling Capacity:

  • Each glass layer increases total storage by 18,000 mB.
  • Each glass layer adds 9 processing slots in the controller inventory.
Step 2: Casting & Pressing Parts

To manufacture tool parts, you must pour molten metals into molds:

  1. Spout Casting: Place the mold under a Create Spout.
    • Handles, Bindings & Sword Bindings require 144 mB of metal.
    • Tool Heads require 288 mB of metal.
  2. Pressing: Put the filled casting mold under a Create Mechanical Press. This extracts the finished Modular Part and spits back your empty mold.
Step 3: Tool Assembly & Replacement

Assemble your modular parts at a standard Smithing Table:

  • Assembly: Place the Binding in the first (Template) slot, the Head in the second (Base) slot, and the Handle in the third (Addition) slot. This constructs the new Modular Tool.
  • Replacing Parts: Place the new part in the Template slot and your tool in the Base slot. This swaps out the old part for the new one.

4. Reforges & Material Modifiers

Reforges

Reforges are permanent enhancements for modular tools. Each tool can have only one at a time.

How to apply: Place your tool, a Modular Template, and a Reforge Stone into the Reforge Table. Left-click with Modular Hammer to apply the reforge once all items are present.
