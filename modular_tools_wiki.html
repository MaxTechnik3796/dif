1. General Information

Welcome to the Modular Tools system of TheDifferential. Instead of crafting traditional tools, this mod allows you to design custom tools by combining different parts (Head, Binding, and Handle) made of various materials.

Each tool's characteristics depend on the materials used to construct its parts:

  • Tool Head: Determines the base tool class (e.g. Pickaxe, Sword, Excavator), its base mining speed (Efficiency), attack damage, and contributes the majority of durability.
  • Binding: Bonds the head and handle together, providing modifier options and additional durability.
  • Handle: Determines the durability multiplier and tool handling characteristics.

Every material possesses a unique Material Trait (passive modifier) that applies automatically to your tool as long as a part made of that material is installed.

2. Tool Crafting & Smelting

Step 1: The Forge Furnace (Multiblock Smeltery)

Before assembling tools, you must melt raw resources into molten metals. This is done inside the Forge Furnace, a multiblock structure of variable height powered by Blaze Burners.

Structure Requirements (Bottom to Top):

  1. Blaze Burner Layer (Y+0): A 3x3 layout of 9 Blaze Burners. Heat determines processing speed and allowed tiers (Kindled = Tier 0-1, Seething = Tier 2-3).
  2. Controller Layer (Y+1): 1 Forge Controller block placed on the front face, surrounded by 8 Forge Bricks (forming a 3x3 layer).
  3. Glass Layers (Y+2+): 3x3 layers of Forge Glass blocks. You can stack from 1 to 16 glass layers.

Scaling Capacity:

  • Each glass layer increases total storage by 18,000 mB (up to 32 fluid tanks total).
  • Each glass layer adds 9 processing slots in the controller inventory. Right-click the controller with items to insert them.
Step 2: Casting & Pressing Parts

To manufacture tool parts, you must pour molten metals into molds using Create mechanics:

  1. Craft Molds: Craft the base Casting Mold (Brass sheets + Brass ingots diagonally). Put it into a Stonecutter to carve specific part molds (e.g. Pickaxe Head Mold, Handle Mold, etc.).
  2. Spout Casting: Place the mold under a Create Spout. Supply the spout with molten metal (drained from the Forge Furnace).
    • Handles, Bindings & Sword Bindings require 144 mB of metal.
    • Tool Heads require 288 mB of metal.
  3. Pressing: Put the filled casting mold under a Create Mechanical Press. This extracts the finished Modular Part and spits back your empty mold.
Step 3: Tool Assembly & Replacement

Assemble your modular parts at a standard Smithing Table:

  • Assembly: Place the Binding in the first (Template) slot, the Head in the second (Base) slot, and the Handle in the third (Addition) slot. This constructs the new Modular Tool.
  • Replacing Parts: If a part is broken or you want to upgrade it, place the new Modular Part in the first (Template) slot, your existing Modular Tool in the second (Base) slot, and leave the third slot empty. This swaps out the old part for the new one.

4. Reforges & Material Traits

Reforging (Reforge Table)

The Reforge Table block allows you to apply a random reforge modifier to a tool. Right-click the Reforge Table and fill all three slots:

  • Slot 1 (Left): Modular Tool.
  • Slot 2 (Middle): Modular Template.
  • Slot 3 (Right): Reforge Stone.

Once filled, the table will consume the template and reforge stone to apply one of the following random prefixes:
